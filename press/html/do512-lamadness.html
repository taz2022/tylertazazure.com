La Madness

LA MADNESS, a talented Minneapolis-based band who perform indie grind blues, blow audiences away with their signature style and live show reputation of “going all the way.” Members are Kurt Vatland, singer/songwriter/guitar; Aaron Willey, drums; David Kittelson, bass; and Tyler 'Taz' Azure, lead guitar and background vocals. Taking the music world by the hair with the release of their 2013 debut album entitled Chances Are, La Madness has gone international with their new breed of indie rock, blues and alternative/grunge. Written by vocalist/guitarist Kurt Vatland, produced/engineered by Bryan Mengy (New Medicine) and mixed/mastered by Colt Leeb (Soul Asylum), this album release has showed audiences that there is indeed something powerful in the music of their band. Adding to the growing buzz surrounding La Madness, Playboy's Jessica Wiuff (Zelinske) graces the cover of the album and band merchandise. La Madness has had the honor to have been supported by bands of merit such as Papa Roach, Glamour of the Kill, Fuel, Tantric, and Stone Senate. With highlighted performances at venues such as the legendary Whisky A Go Go in Hollywood, House of Blues in San Diego, Count's Vamp'd in Las Vegas, Joe's Grottos in Phoenix, Herman's Hideaway in Denver, as well as Minneapolis' famed hot spot such as First Avenue-7th Street Entry, the band is cultivating an ever- growing loyal fan base. In response to this, in October of 2013 the band released Episode #1 of their Rockumentary Series 'Going Mad', which featured their first music video for “Got Me Wrong.” Most recently, their independent WHITEOUT TOUR started with a European leg, which included a performance with Papa Roach in Germany, and then continued with the band hurtling across America, cataloging more than 20,000 miles in total without even a record label. But it won’t be long. LA rock journalist and radio personality Paige Montgomery (www.lamusicblog.com) perhaps said it best when she described La Madness as "what would happen if Nirvana and Buckcherry had an illegitimate child." Reviews such as this earned them airtime support from a variety of radio stations such as KROQ in Los Angeles, 93X in Minneapolis, KPRI in San Diego, KWSS in Phoenix, and Rockn 101 in St Cloud. Recent accolades include being awarded Best Minnesota Band of 2013 by Rockn 101 FM, Minnesota HomeGrown Band of July, and having their first music video for “Got Me Wrong” being selected as one of CityPages.com Top 10 Must See Minnesota Music Videos. La Madness/Kurt Vatland is licensed BMI and a member of the Record Artists Guild of Los Angeles, CA.
